Executive Summary
“This Brand Book is Hemut’s single source of truth 
for voice, messaging, and visual identity. It exists 
so every team (marketing, sales, product, design, 
partners, and more) speaks and looks like one 
company and that every customer interaction 
supports our promise: Take Back Your Margins. 
For all on those teams, consider this your bible.
The book is organized into five practical sections. 
Brand Core defines our mission, promise, values, 
and the hero we serve. Visual Identity contains 
our logo system, color palette, typography, 
iconography, and image guidance you must use 
to stay consistent. Messaging is the operational 
playbook for sales and marketing: our StoryBrand 
Brandscript and a soundbite bank for hero lines, 
CTAs, email subjects, and sales openers. 
Applications shows concrete templates and 
rules for website, product UI, pitch decks, social, 
print materials, and merchandise. Governance 
explains where files live, the approval flow, 
versioning conventions, and who to contact for 
exceptions. Combined, these five sections equip 
you with everything you need to make compelling 
content that fits Hemut’s brand.
How should you use the Brand Book? Treat it like 
the rulebook for a board game. Every act you take 
as part of playing the game (making content for 
Hemut) must fall in line with the rules set by the 
book. The best players understand the rules and 
then get creative within them to develop unique 
strategies and ideas. That should be you. 
Understand the rules and follow them, but not to 
the point of rigidity. Be creative and unique in 
what you ultimately create.
This Brand Book is a living tool, not a theory. Copy 
and paste the soundbites, apply the color palette 
and assets, and use the templates so work looks 
and reads like Hemut everywhere. If you’re unsure 
where to start, what to use, or if you need help, 
contact the Head of Marketing, Aidan Tack, for a 
quick review. Use these rules to make Hemut 
unmistakable: consistent, credible, and 
relentlessly focused on helping customers 
keep more profit on every mile.”
This Brand Book exists to serve as Hemut’s single source of truth for voice, messaging, 
and visuals. Use it to make every customer touchpoint unmistakably Hemut.

Our Audiences
Mid Market Mikey
Mid Market Mikey represents the ideal customer profile for Hemut’s 
software solutions. Mid Market Mikey is the owner and the CEO or 
COO of his own trucking company. His fleet ranges anywhere from 
200 to 2000 trucks. He’s down to earth, practical, gritty, a grinder, 
and intensely focused on making his family business as successful 
as possible. Often times, he even still has his CDL.
Owner Operator Oliver
Owner Operator Oliver represents the ideal customer profile for 
Hemut Diesel, our own fuel card service. He is the owner of his own 
truck or small fleet, ranging anywhere from 1 to 25 trucks. He’s a 
hustler, extremely blue collar, and no-nonsense. He’s naturally 
skeptical and conservative, but is highly motivated by the prospect of 
making more money off of his truck(s).
General Public
Although Hemut occupies a niche industry, we aim to build a 
recognizable public brand to attract investors, talent, and business 
partnerships. As a result, we’ll be marketing heavily on social media 
and to the general public in order to gain virality and put our story 
out into the world.

Hemut’s messaging strategy exists to make that possible. It 
gives our entire team, from sales to marketing to 
partnerships, a shared language for explaining who we are, 
what we do, and why it matters to the people we serve.

This messaging strategy is built on the 7-Part StoryBrand 
framework, a proven system for turning a company’s story 
into a clear and repeatable narrative. It helps us move from 
talking about features to telling a story our customers 
instantly recognize themselves in.

A story that positions our customers as the hero, not us.

Consistency builds trust. When every team member speaks 
the same language and tells the same story, we create clarity 
in the market and confidence with our customers. 

Therefore, this section of the brand book exists to:
Align all Hemut communication under one clear narrative.
Make our story repeatable across every channel.
Equip anyone (writer, designer, or salesperson) to speak with the 
same confident voice.

Think of our messaging strategy as your playbook for 
storytelling:
   • If you’re writing website copy, use it to frame headlines and calls 
to action.
   • If you’re creating sales material, use it to build customer-centered 
narratives.
   • If you’re leading a conversation, use it to talk about Hemut in a 
way that resonates and converts.

When everyone at Hemut uses the same language, we 
become louder, clearer, and more believable; and our 
customers know exactly what we stand for. 

TLDR:  This strategy ensures that every piece of communication, from a 
homepage banner to a handshake pitch, tells the same story.
Every company tells a story, but only a few tell it clearly 
enough for everyone to repeat it the same way.

Brand Story
We transform the heads of America’s best fleets from margin-squeezed firefighters into strategic leaders 
who earn more money on every mile.

The final transformation describes who Mid Market Mikey can become with Hemut’s help. Its role is psychological 
and practical; it turns abstract benefits into a believable new identity the customer wants to occupy. Rather than 
listing features or outcomes, the transformation shows the identify shift, emotional payoff, and day-to-day 
benefits they’ll gain. A clear transformation motivates action because people buy into a future version of 
themselves: it showcases the image that Mid Market Mikey will try to inhabit and the reasons he’ll choose to 
change. 
The Call to Action is the point in our story where we as the guide hand Mid Market Mikey a clear, low-friction next 
step. Its job is purely practical: remove uncertainty, lower the cost of trying us, and make the path forward to 
doing business obvious. We split the Call-to-Action into two complementary types. The Direct CTA is for buyers 
who are ready to engage now. It should be short, imperative, and outcome-oriented. The Transitional CTA is for 
audiences who need education or proof before they’ll speak to sales. It’s meant to built trust, show our 
competency, and keep the customer engaged.
